# Sri Lankan Market Context - EcoHabit

## Market Overview

### Waste Management Crisis in Sri Lanka
- **Daily Waste Generation**: 7,000 metric tons per day
- **Per Capita**: 1.4 kg per person per day
- **Collection Efficiency**: Only 50% of generated waste is collected
- **Disposal Methods**: 65% open dumping, 20% landfills, 10% composting, 5% recycling

### Environmental Impact
- **Meethotamulla Disaster**: 2017 landfill collapse killed 32 people
- **Health Impacts**: Dengue outbreaks linked to poor waste management
- **Coastal Pollution**: Plastic waste affecting marine ecosystems
- **Water Contamination**: Groundwater pollution from open dumps

## Target Demographics

### Primary Market (Urban Areas)
- **Age**: 18-40 years
- **Income**: Middle to upper-middle class
- **Education**: High school to university level
- **Technology**: High smartphone penetration (75-85% in urban areas)
- **Environmental Awareness**: Growing concern about environmental issues

### Secondary Market (Suburban Areas)
- **Age**: 25-45 years
- **Income**: Middle class
- **Education**: High school level
- **Technology**: Moderate smartphone usage
- **Environmental Awareness**: Basic understanding, needs education

### Tertiary Market (Rural Areas)
- **Age**: 20-50 years
- **Income**: Lower-middle class
- **Education**: Primary to high school level
- **Technology**: Basic smartphone usage
- **Environmental Awareness**: Limited, needs significant education

## Waste Categories & Disposal Methods

### Official Waste Categories
1. **Organic/Biodegradable (60%)**
   - Food waste, garden waste, agricultural waste
   - Disposal: Composting, anaerobic digestion, open dumping

2. **Plastic (12%)**
   - Bottles, bags, packaging, containers
   - Disposal: Open dumping, limited recycling, incineration

3. **Paper & Cardboard (12%)**
   - Newspapers, magazines, packaging, office paper
   - Disposal: Open dumping, limited recycling, burning

4. **E-waste (1.5%)**
   - Electronic devices, batteries, appliances
   - Disposal: Open dumping, limited formal collection

5. **Glass (3%)**
   - Bottles, jars, containers
   - Disposal: Open dumping, limited recycling

6. **Metal (2%)**
   - Cans, containers, scrap metal
   - Disposal: Open dumping, limited recycling

### E-Waste Specific Categories
- **Computers & Laptops**: 40% of e-waste
- **Mobile Phones**: 25% of e-waste
- **Televisions**: 20% of e-waste
- **Other Electronics**: 15% of e-waste

### Household Segregation
- **Current Practice**: Minimal segregation at household level
- **Barriers**: Lack of awareness, limited collection infrastructure
- **Opportunities**: Education and gamification can improve segregation

## Market Opportunities & Gaps

### Current Gaps
1. **Household Segregation**: Minimal awareness and practice
2. **E-Waste Collection**: Limited formal collection infrastructure
3. **Public Education**: Insufficient environmental awareness
4. **Incentive Systems**: No gamification or reward mechanisms
5. **Community Engagement**: Limited social aspects of waste management

### Opportunities
1. **Mobile-First Approach**: High smartphone penetration enables app-based solutions
2. **Gamification**: No existing gamified waste management apps
3. **Education**: Significant need for environmental education
4. **Community Building**: Social aspects can drive engagement
5. **Local Partnerships**: Collaboration with existing recyclers

### Competitive Landscape
- **No Direct Competitors**: No gamified waste management apps in Sri Lanka
- **Traditional Methods**: Manual waste collection and disposal
- **Government Initiatives**: Limited digital solutions
- **NGO Programs**: Basic awareness campaigns, no gamification

## Regulatory Framework

### National Policy on Waste Management
- **Vision**: Zero waste to landfills by 2030
- **Strategy**: Reduce, reuse, recycle approach
- **Implementation**: Provincial and local government responsibility

### E-Waste Management Regulations
- **Extended Producer Responsibility**: Manufacturers responsible for end-of-life products
- **Licensing**: 16 licensed e-waste collectors
- **Data Security**: Requirements for data destruction
- **Environmental Standards**: Proper disposal and recycling standards

### Local Government Regulations
- **Waste Collection**: Municipal responsibility
- **Fees**: User fees for waste collection services
- **Penalties**: Fines for improper waste disposal
- **Incentives**: Limited incentive programs
